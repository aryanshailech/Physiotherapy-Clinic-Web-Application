About Us:

Our History

Agrawal Physiotherapy Clinic was established in 2005 with a mission to provide high-quality physiotherapy services to our community. Our founder, Dr. Shailesh Agrawal, envisioned a place where patients could receive personalized care tailored to their unique needs. Starting with a small team of dedicated professionals, the clinic quickly grew in reputation due to its effective treatment methods and patient-centric approach.

Over the years, the clinic has expanded its services to include advanced rehabilitation programs, sports physiotherapy, and pediatric care. We have invested in state-of-the-art equipment and continuous staff training to ensure we remain at the forefront of physiotherapy practice. Today, Agrawal Physiotherapy Clinic is recognized as a leading provider of physiotherapy services in the region, known for our commitment to improving patient outcomes and quality of life.

Founder

Dr. Shailesh Agrawal

Dr. Shailesh Agrawal, a highly experienced physiotherapist, founded the clinic with a vision to offer personalized and effective treatment to patients. With over 20 years of experience in the field, Dr. Agrawal has been instrumental in the clinic's success.

Dr. Agrawal graduated from GMC Nagpur with a degree in Physiotherapy and further specialized in orthopedic physiotherapy. He has been actively involved in research and has published numerous papers on innovative physiotherapy techniques. His dedication to patient care and continuous learning has earned him respect and recognition within the medical community.

Under his leadership, Agrawal Physiotherapy Clinic has received multiple awards and accolades, solidifying its reputation as a trusted healthcare provider. Dr. Agrawal continues to inspire his team with his passion for excellence and unwavering commitment to patient well-being.

Modern Equipments

Ultrasound Therapy Machine

An ultrasound machine for physiotherapy is a medical device used to deliver therapeutic ultrasound waves to the body for the treatment of musculoskeletal conditions. It is commonly used by physiotherapists to help reduce pain, inflammation, and muscle spasms, as well as to promote tissue healing..

Interferential Therapy Unit (I.F.T) with Transcutaneous electrical nerve stimulator (T.E.N.S)

When using an IFT with TENS unit, the therapist will typically place the electrodes on or near the area of pain or discomfort. The device allows for various settings to be adjusted, including frequency, intensity, and duration of treatment, depending on the individual's condition and needs.

Short Wave Diathermy (S.W.D)

Short Wave Diathermy (SWD) is a therapeutic modality used in physiotherapy for the treatment of various musculoskeletal conditions. It involves the use of high-frequency electromagnetic energy to generate heat deep within the tissues, providing pain relief and promoting healing.

Traction

There are several types of traction machines used in physiotherapy to apply traction to the spine or extremities. These machines are designed to provide controlled and adjustable pulling forces to achieve therapeutic goals such as decompression, pain relief, and improved joint mobility.
